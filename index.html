How it works

No cloud. Three steps.

  1. 1

    Hold the hotkey and talk

    Freeflow records only while you hold the key. Release to stop.

  2. 2

    Your machine transcribes it

    whisper.cpp turns your voice into text right on your computer. Nothing is uploaded.

  3. 3

    Clean text lands at your cursor

    A small local AI tidies the words, then Freeflow types the result where you were.

Features

Everything runs on your machine.

AI cleanup

Fillers gone, punctuation fixed, capitalized. You talk loosely, it writes clean.

Tones per app

Formal for email, casual for chat. Freeflow picks the tone by the app you type into.

Personal dictionary

Force the spellings you care about. Names, tools, jargon land right every time.

Local history

Your recent dictations stay on disk, yours to keep or wipe. Never synced anywhere.

Dark mode

A settings app that matches your desktop, light or dark.

Tray and hotkey

Lives in your system tray. One chord to dictate, hands-free mode on a double tap.

Install

Up and running in three commands.

$ git clone https://github.com/joshazmy/freeflow
$ ./install.sh          # prints a plan, asks once, installs locally
$ freeflow onboarding    # 6-step first-run setup

Fedora, Arch and Debian friendly · MIT · uninstall.sh removes every trace.
